Process

In its broadest meaning, self-directed learning describes a process in which individuals take the initiative, with or without the help of others, in diagnosing their learning needs, formulating learning goals, identifying human and material resources for learning, choosing and implementing appropriate learning strategies, and evaluating learning outcomes.

Key process details

The process is executed through the following:

1. A facilitator who possesses the following key skills and is in the process of developing them further:

hearing

a) Listening - a facilitator offers deep and neutral listening.

update

b) Managing Processes such as managing time, setting group norms, sensing the energy of the group and responding appropriately

c) Asking questions with the intention of enabling the emergence of new knowledge for the individual and/or group. The emergent knowledge has often been experienced to be varied for different participants in a group.

drag_indicator

d) Synthesis - It is the combination of components or elements which form a connected whole. It incorporates the facts/beliefs shared by the participants, and is offered at the end of every session.

2. Group of 10

The size of the group is determined with the intention to ensure individualised attention to each of the 10 participants in the group.

3. Curriculum of questions to enable the practice of reflection, action and silence

The curriculum consists of a series of questions centred around the following meta-themes:

fingerprint

Learning to be

search

Learning to learn

Learning to do

Learning to live together

These questions are used as a means to enable initiation into and deepening of a practice of reflection, action and silence.

Context

As teenagers, when we transition into a life that requires of us to make informed choices in life, career or relationship paths, we are faced with situations that requires us to state our purpose ( think of statements of purpose), our strengths (think of interviews and applications) and choosing fields ( think of humanities, sciences etc.). But do we know ourselves enough to make these choices authentically?

We wish to be better learners with an ability to critically think, concentrate and innovate(like the dedicated maestros) while also being able to go out-share the word, understand perspectives and collaborate(like empathetic leaders) to bring ideas to action. All this time, being closest to our essence, in harmony and in peace, within and without.

But does the world nurture us to develop and build our own conscious thought and skills which are critical in the 21st century? What prepares us to face change and ambiguity with aplomb?

Ikigai is a japanese concept meaning a reason for being.

At Creatnet, we equip young leaders with the tools of Learning to Be, Do, Learn and Live and work together, from the ancient to the modern, that empower them to discover their Ikigai.



Reclaiming learning



what should be the process of learning, what should i learn, how should i learn, what should be the goal of learning, and several other related questions related to learning are often outsourced for contemplation and reflection to institutions and/or other individuals. We are exploring a dynamic path where we can ask these questions of ourselves, and progress on the path which emerges post reflection.

Learning goals



The learning process is designed to foster a unique articulation of well-being and growth for participants(and for the groups and systems they are a part of). It will contribute in varying degrees to the physical, emotional, spiritual and academic/professional well-being and growth.

Broader Goals:

Managing self and time ( socio-emotional, physical well being)

Experience of work aligned with their nature, strengths and the world's needs

Clarity of career and life path
